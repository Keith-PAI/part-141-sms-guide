1 What the Report Proposes

The NFTA report outlines five major areas of regulatory modernization for Part 141 flight schools:

Proposal A

Central Management Office (CMO)

Centralized certification nationwide, replacing uneven local FSDO interpretations with consistent national standards for Part 141 oversight.

Proposal B

Mandatory SMS for All Part 141 Schools

Formal Safety Management Systems would be required—not optional. Every certificated school would need a functioning SMS aligned with ICAO standards.

Proposal C

Two-Tier Quality Standards

Schools must demonstrate both documented procedures AND measurable results—going beyond paperwork to provide evidence that your processes actually work.

Proposal D

Expanded Simulation & XR Credit

New credit allowances for flight simulation devices, extended reality (XR) training, and a new Enhanced Advanced Aviation Training Device (EAATD) category.

Proposal E

Overhauled Examining Authority

Pass-rate thresholds replaced by system maturity metrics, instructor standardization requirements, and internal evaluation capabilities.

2 What SMS Would Mean for Your School

The Four Pillars of SMS

📋

Safety Policy

Management commitment, safety objectives, accountable executive designation

⚠️

Safety Risk Management

Hazard identification, risk analysis, risk assessment and mitigation

📊

Safety Assurance

Safety performance monitoring, management of change, continuous improvement

🎓

Safety Promotion

Safety training, safety communication, building a positive safety culture

Typical Documentation Required

  • Safety Management System manual
  • Safety policy statement signed by accountable executive
  • Hazard register and risk assessment records
  • Safety performance indicators (SPIs) and targets
  • Safety reporting forms and investigation procedures
  • Safety training records and curricula
  • Management review and audit records

Common Gaps in Flight School Safety Cultures

  • No formal hazard reporting system (or one that exists on paper but isn't used)
  • Safety policy that hasn't been reviewed or updated in years
  • No designated accountable executive or safety officer
  • Reactive approach—only addressing safety after incidents occur
  • No tracking of safety metrics or performance indicators
  • Lack of regular safety meetings or safety promotion activities
  • Training records not standardized across instructors
